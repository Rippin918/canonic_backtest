Anti-Patterns — What Kills Profitability

k=10 + AS Optimal = Death. High order intensity decay (k=10) combined with the pure A-S spread formula creates quotes so wide that nothing fills. This consistently produced zero-trade or negative configs across all pairs. The lesson: on a thin book like Canonic, you need either aggressive k (low values like 1.5) with optimal spreads, or defensive k (high values) with Fibonacci-adaptive spreads that tighten opportunistically. Never combine high k with raw A-S.
q_max=10 without rebalancing (threshold=1.0) on CANONIC pairs is risky. The low liquidity means inventory can build up fast with no natural exit. The rebalance threshold at 0.85 consistently outperformed 1.0 (no rebalance) on CANONIC pairs, while making less difference on WETH/USDm.
